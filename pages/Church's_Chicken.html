Church's Chicken is a U.S. chain of fast food restaurants specializing in fried chicken founded by George W. Church, Sr. on April 17, 1952 in San Antonio , across the street from The Alamo . The company now has more than 1,500 locations worldwide. It is well-known for serving chicken with a side of hot peppers . Church's openly admits concentrating on areas with high proportions of African Americans in the population, stating that research shows that they are indeed the most avid consumers of fried chicken and it is not merely a case of ethnic stereotyping . In some areas it is co-franchised with the White Castle hamburger chain. Church's is now owned by AFC Enterprises , along with Popeye's Chicken & Biscuits and Cinnabon . Hala Moddelmog was appointed as president of Church's Chicken in 1996 , making her the first female president of a fast-food restaurant . Church’s Chicken began its corporate partnership with The Children’s Miracle Network in the summer of 2004 .
